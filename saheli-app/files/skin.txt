Phase 1: The Daily Check-In (Data Collection)
Since skin conditions vary wildly (Acne vs. Eczema vs. Moles), the app should first ask: "What are we tracking today?" (General Health, specific Lesion/Mole, or a Flare-up).

A. The "Vitals" (Sensory & Visual)
Itch Level (Pruritus): "On a scale of 1-10, how itchy is your skin today?"

Why: Intense itch disturbs sleep and indicates active inflammation (Eczema/Psoriasis).

Pain/Tenderness: "Does the area hurt when you touch it?"

Options: No / A little / Throbbing pain (Red Flag for abscess/cyst).

Heat Check (Crucial): "Does the red area feel hotter than the surrounding skin?"

Why: Heat = Infection (Cellulitis). This is a medical urgency.

Texture: "How does the skin feel?"

Options: Smooth / Dry & Flaky / Rough & Bumpy / Weeping (Oozing fluid).

B. The "Lesion" Tracker (For specific spots/moles)
If the user is tracking a specific spot, use the ABCDE Rule logic:
5.  Change in Size: "Does the spot look bigger today compared to the last photo?"
6.  Change in Color: "Has it changed color (e.g., turned black, purple, or multi-colored)?"
7.  Bleeding: "Did it bleed spontaneously (without you scratching it)?" (Red Flag).

C. Contextual Triggers
Skincare Routine: "Did you use any new products (soaps, lotions, makeup) in the last 3 days?"

Why: Contact dermatitis (allergic reaction) often has a delayed onset.

Dietary Triggers: "Did you consume high-sugar or high-dairy foods yesterday?" (Common acne triggers).

Stress & Sleep: "Rate your stress level." (Stress releases cortisol, which causes oil production and inflammation).

Phase 2: AI Logic & Triage (The "Brain")
🟢 Zone 1: Stable / Improving
Conditions: Itch score < 3, no pain, no heat, lesions are drying up or fading.

AI Interpretation: The skin barrier is healing.

App Message: "Your skin seems calm today."

Suggestion: "Consistency is key. Apply your moisturizer/sunscreen as usual. Don't pick at healing spots!"

🟡 Zone 2: Flare-Up (Inflammation)
Conditions: Itch score > 5, new redness, new bumps (papules/pustules), or increased dryness.

AI Interpretation: Active inflammation (Acne breakout, Eczema flare, or Rosacea).

App Message: "It looks like you're having a flare-up."

Suggestion:

Action: "Simplify your routine today. Stick to a gentle cleanser and moisturizer only. Avoid active ingredients (like retinol/acids) on irritated skin."

Lifestyle: "Change your pillowcase tonight. Avoid hot water when washing your face."

🔴 Zone 3: Infection or Danger (Medical Attention Needed)
Conditions:

Heat: Skin feels hot to the touch.

Spread: Redness is expanding rapidly (drawing a line around the redness is a common doctor trick—if it crosses the line, it's spreading).

Systemic Signs: User reports Fever + Rash.

Angioedema: Swelling of the lips, eyes, or tongue.

AI Interpretation: Risk of Cellulitis (bacterial infection), severe allergic reaction, or viral exanthem.

App Message: "ALERT: POTENTIAL INFECTION OR SEVERE REACTION."

Suggestion: "Please see a doctor immediately. If you have trouble breathing or your throat feels tight, go to the ER."

Phase 3: Specialized Women's Health Features
Skin is the dashboard of a woman's hormonal health.

1. The "Hormonal Acne" Predictor
The Logic: Testosterone and Progesterone fluctuate during the cycle.

Ovulation (Day 14): Skin is often "glowing" (Estrogen peak).

Luteal Phase (Day 21-28): Progesterone rises, causing oil glands to close up. This is when deep, painful cysts appear on the jawline.

AI Action: Sync with her cycle.

Suggestion (Day 20): "Your cycle is entering the Luteal phase. Your skin is producing more oil now. Start using a Salicylic Acid cleanser proactively to prevent next week's breakout."

2. Pregnancy Mode (The "Glow" vs. The "Mask")
Melasma: Dark patches on the face ("Mask of Pregnancy").

Suggestion: "Your skin is highly sensitive to sun right now due to hormones. You MUST wear SPF 50+ even indoors."

PUPPP Rash: An intensely itchy rash on the belly during late pregnancy.

AI Alert: If a pregnant user reports "Intense Itch on Belly," the app should suggest: "This could be PUPPP or Cholestasis (liver issue). Mention this to your OB-GYN immediately."

3. PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) Detection
If the user consistently logs Acne on the jawline + Excess hair growth (Hirsutism) + Irregular Periods, the AI should recognize the pattern.

Suggestion: "You have logged consistent jawline breakouts and irregular cycles. Have you discussed PCOS with your doctor? This is often a hormonal issue, not just a skin issue."

Phase 4: Pattern Recognition (The AI's Value Add)
"Purging" vs. "Breaking Out":

Scenario: User starts a new Retinol product.

Pattern: Skin gets worse for 2-4 weeks, then better.

AI Insight: "This looks like 'purging' (increasing cell turnover), which is normal for this product. Stick with it unless it itches/burns."

Contrast: If the reaction is in a new area where she never gets acne, the AI says: "This is likely a reaction, not purging. Stop the product."

The "Dairy/Sugar" Lag:

Acne from diet doesn't happen immediately; it often takes 48-72 hours.

AI Insight: "You're breaking out today (Wednesday). You logged high sugar intake on Sunday. This might be the delayed trigger."

The "Stress" Flare:

Pattern: User logs "High Stress" on Monday; Eczema/Psoriasis flares on Wednesday.

AI Insight: "Stress is a major trigger for your eczema. Consider mindfulness techniques when you feel your stress levels rising to prevent skin flares."

Summary for the App Developer
Visual Tracking: Allow users to upload photos to compare Day 1 vs Day 30 (Use a "Ghost" overlay so they take the photo at the same angle).

Cycle Syncing: Absolute necessity for female users.

The "Heat" Question: This is your safety net against missing dangerous infections.

Disclaimer: "AI cannot diagnose cancer or infection definitively. If a mole changes or a rash spreads, see a dermatologist."
