Phase 1: The Daily Check-In (Data Collection)
Context is everything. A blood sugar of 140 mg/dL is perfect after a meal, but high if you haven't eaten all day. The AI must ask "When?" before analyzing "How much?"

A. The "Vitals" (The Numbers)
Glucose Reading: [Numeric Input] (Support mg/dL and mmol/L).

Timing Tag (The User MUST select one):

Fasting (Just woke up).

Pre-Meal (Before eating).

Post-Meal (1-2 hours after eating).

Bedtime.

B. The "Red Flag" Symptom Check
Ask these questions to categorize the danger level.

Hypoglycemia (Low Sugar) Symptoms:
3.  "Do you feel shaky or tingling hands, sweaty, or have a racing heart?"
4.  "Are you feeling suddenly intensely hungry or irritable ('Hangry')?"
5.  "Do you feel confused or lightheaded?" (Red Flag: Brain is starving).

Hyperglycemia (High Sugar) Symptoms:
6.  "Are you thirstier than usual (Polydipsia)?"
7.  "Are you urinating frequently (Polyuria)?"
8.  "Is your vision blurry?"
9.  "Do you feel nauseous or have stomach pain?" (Red Flag: DKA risk).

C. Contextual Factors
Carb Intake: "Did you have a heavy meal (pasta, rice, sweets) recently?"

Activity: "Did you exercise today?" (Exercise usually lowers blood sugar).

Insulin/Meds: "Did you take your dose?"

Phase 2: AI Logic & Triage (The "Brain")
Note: These ranges vary slightly by patient, but these are standard clinical safety zones.

🔴 Zone 1: Hypoglycemia (THE DANGER ZONE)
Reading: < 70 mg/dL (< 3.9 mmol/L).

Symptoms: Shaking, sweating, confusion.

AI Interpretation: Immediate Medical Emergency. The brain lacks fuel.

App Action: FLASHING RED ALERT.

Immediate Suggestion: "Your sugar is dangerously low. Rule of 15:"

Eat 15g of fast-acting carbs (4 oz juice, 1 tbsp honey, or 3-4 glucose tablets).

Wait 15 minutes.

Re-check. If still low, repeat.

If < 55 mg/dL: "Call for help immediately or use your Glucagon kit if you have one."

🟢 Zone 2: Target Range (Safe)
Fasting/Pre-Meal: 80 – 130 mg/dL.

Post-Meal (2 hrs): < 180 mg/dL.

AI Interpretation: Excellent control.

App Message: "You are in the green zone."

Suggestion: "Keep up the good work. Logging this helps your doctor see your stability."

🟡 Zone 3: Elevated (Warning)
Reading: 180 – 250 mg/dL.

AI Interpretation: Insulin resistance is high, or medication was missed/insufficient for the meal eaten.

App Message: "Your sugar is running high."

Suggestion:

"Drink a large glass of water to help flush out excess sugar."

"If you feel well, take a 15-minute gentle walk to lower levels naturally."

"Review your last meal—was it high in hidden sugars?"

🟠 Zone 4: Severe Hyperglycemia (Ketosis Risk)
Reading: > 250 mg/dL (on two consecutive readings).

Symptoms: Nausea, fruit-smelling breath, extreme thirst.

AI Interpretation: Risk of Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA), a life-threatening condition where blood turns acidic.

App Message: "WARNING: HIGH RISK."

Suggestion:

"Check for Ketones (urine strip) immediately if you have Type 1 Diabetes."

"Do NOT exercise (this can spike sugar further if ketones are present)."

"Contact your doctor."

Phase 3: Women's Specific Features (Crucial)
Women with diabetes face unique challenges due to hormones.

1. The "Luteal Phase" Spike (Premenstrual)
The Science: Progesterone peaks the week before a period. Progesterone causes insulin resistance.

The AI Logic: If the user logs "Period starting in 3 days," the AI should expect higher blood sugar numbers (e.g., fasting 110-120 instead of 90).

Suggestion: "You are in the pre-menstrual phase. Your blood sugar may run higher than usual regardless of diet. Be patient with yourself, and ask your doctor if you need a temporary insulin increase."

2. PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) Mode
Many women have both Diabetes and PCOS.

The AI Logic: Tracks weight and sugar together.

Suggestion: "Focus on protein and healthy fats. Insulin resistance is higher with PCOS, so 'carb counting' is critical."

3. Gestational Diabetes (Pregnancy Mode)
The Targets Change: The limits are MUCH stricter to protect the baby.

Fasting > 95 mg/dL is considered HIGH (vs 130 in non-pregnant).

1-hour post-meal > 140 mg/dL is HIGH.

AI Action: If the user toggles "Pregnant," the App checks against these stricter thresholds.

Suggestion: "High sugar crosses the placenta. Stick to the diet plan rigidly for the baby's safety."

Phase 4: Pattern Recognition (The AI's Value Add)
The App should look for these three common diabetic phenomena:

The "Dawn Phenomenon":

Pattern: Sugar is normal at bedtime but High (e.g., 160) immediately upon waking up, without eating.

AI Insight: "Your liver is dumping sugar while you sleep. Discuss this morning spike with your doctor; you might need a medication adjustment."

The "Somogyi Effect" (Rebound):

Pattern: User has a Low (Hypo) at 3 AM, followed by a High (Hyper) at 7 AM.

AI Insight: "Your body panicked from the low sugar overnight and overcompensated. You might be taking too much evening insulin or need a bedtime snack."

The "Pizza Effect" (Delayed Spike):

Pattern: User eats pizza/burgers. Sugar is fine 1 hour later, but spikes dangerously 4-5 hours later.

AI Insight: "High fat foods delay sugar absorption. Next time, check your sugar 4 hours after a heavy meal, not just 2."
