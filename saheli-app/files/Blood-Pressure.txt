B. The "Red Flag" Symptom Check
If the numbers are high, or even if they are normal, ask these questions to detect target organ damage.

Headache: "Do you have a headache today?"

Options: No / Mild / Severe & Throbbing (Red Flag) / Thunderclap (Emergency).

Vision Changes: "Is your vision normal?"

Options: Yes / Blurry / I see spots or 'floaters' (Red Flag).

Chest & Breathing: "Any discomfort in the chest or trouble breathing?"

Options: No / Shortness of breath / Chest pain or tightness (Emergency).

Neurological: "Do you feel any weakness on one side of the body or difficulty speaking?"

Options: No / Yes (Stroke Alert - Emergency).

C. Contextual Factors
Salt Intake: "Did you consume a high-salt meal yesterday or today?" (Fast food, pickles, processed meat).

Medication: "Did you take your BP medication today?"

Stress Level: "Rate your stress today (1-10)."

Phase 2: AI Logic & Triage (The "Brain")
This logic combines the Numbers (JNC-8/AHA Guidelines) with the Symptoms.

🟢 Zone 1: Optimal / Normal
Reading: Systolic < 120 AND Diastolic < 80.

Symptoms: None.

AI Interpretation: Cardiovascular system is under low stress.

App Suggestion: "Perfect reading. Maintain your current lifestyle."

Tip: "Great job. Keep your sodium intake low to stay in this zone."

🟡 Zone 2: Elevated / Stage 1 (The "Warning Zone")
Reading: Systolic 130–139 OR Diastolic 80–89.

Symptoms: None or mild headache.

AI Interpretation: The arteries are under pressure. If this persists, it damages blood vessels.

App Suggestion: "Your BP is slightly elevated today."

Action: "Check your salt intake. Have you been stressed? Try a 10-minute deep breathing session."

Logic: If this persists for >7 days, the App prompts: "Your trends are consistently elevated. Please consult your doctor about adjusting your dosage."

🟠 Zone 3: Stage 2 Hypertension (High Risk)
Reading: Systolic ≥ 140 OR Diastolic ≥ 90.

Symptoms: None.

AI Interpretation: Dangerous if left untreated long-term.

App Suggestion: "Your blood pressure is high."

Action: "Did you miss your medication? Please sit in a quiet room for 15 minutes and re-measure."

Alert: If the re-measure is still high, "Contact your healthcare provider today."

🔴 Zone 4: Hypertensive Crisis (Medical Emergency)
Reading: Systolic > 180 and/or Diastolic > 120.

Symptoms: ANY symptom (Chest pain, severe headache, confusion, vision change).

AI Interpretation: Immediate risk of Stroke or Heart Attack.

App Message: "CRITICAL ALERT: SEEK EMERGENCY CARE."

Display: A giant red button to call Emergency Services or a designated Emergency Contact.

Instruction: "Do not drive yourself to the hospital."

Phase 3: Specialized Women's Health Features
Since you mentioned specializing in women patients, your app needs two distinct modes that generic apps often miss:

1. The "Preeclampsia" Mode (Pregnancy)
If the user profile says she is pregnant, the AI thresholds change.

The Nuance: A BP of 140/90 in a normal person is "Stage 2." In a pregnant woman, it is a potential emergency (Preeclampsia) that threatens both mother and baby.

Additional Question: "Do you have swelling in your face or hands? Is there pain in your upper right belly?"

AI Action: If BP > 140/90 + Swelling = Immediate ER referral.

2. The "Hypotension" Mode (Low BP)
Young women often suffer from Low BP (e.g., 90/60), causing fainting.

Trigger: Systolic < 90 OR Diastolic < 60.

Questions: "Do you feel dizzy? Did you faint?"

Suggestion: "Drink 500ml of water immediately. Add a pinch of salt to your next meal. Lie down with your legs raised to help blood flow to the brain."

Phase 4: Pattern Recognition (The Engineer's Edge)
The AI should identify these specific patterns:

"White Coat" Filtering:

If the user logs "High" at the doctor's office but "Normal" at home consistently, the App notes: "You may have White Coat Hypertension. Show this log to your doctor."

Morning Surge:

If BP is consistently high only in the morning (6 AM - 10 AM), this is a risk factor for morning strokes. The App should suggest: "Ask your doctor if you should take your medication at night instead of the morning."

Salt Sensitivity:

If the user marks "High Salt" on Saturday and BP spikes on Sunday, the AI learns: "Your body is highly salt-sensitive. Pizza seems to trigger your BP spikes."

Summary for the App Developer
Input: 3 numbers (Sys/Dia/Pulse) + 5-6 Yes/No symptom toggles.

Output: Color-coded status (Green/Yellow/Red).

Critical Feature: Differentiate between "High BP" (Chronic issue) and "Crisis" (Immediate danger).
